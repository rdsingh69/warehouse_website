Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about StockFlowTech cold storage warehouse management software — features, setup, customization, billing, and integrations.

Getting Started

Is StockFlowTech production-ready?

Yes. StockFlowTech offers production-grade deployments for cold storage operations. The platform handles inward/outward workflows, stock ownership tracking, rent billing, and payment ledgers in live environments. A guided trial is available so your team can validate fit before full rollout.

How long does it take to set up?

A standard warehouse setup — including warehouses, locations, seasons, products, and user accounts — typically takes 1–3 days. Complex multi-site deployments with custom workflows may take 1–2 weeks. Our team provides onboarding support throughout.

Do I need technical experience to use it?

No. StockFlowTech is designed for warehouse operations teams without IT backgrounds. The interface guides users through inward, outward, and payment flows step by step. Admin setup and configuration is handled during onboarding.

Warehouse & Stock Management

Can I manage multiple warehouses?

Yes. StockFlowTech supports unlimited warehouse facilities under a single account. Each warehouse has its own locations, rent configuration, user roles, and reports. An admin-level dashboard provides consolidated visibility across all sites.

How does stock ownership tracking work?

Every stock lot in the system is linked to a specific farmer or owner. Ownership is recorded at inward entry and automatically updated on transfers, partial dispatches, and sales. Rent charges follow the owner, so billing is always accurate even when stock is split or transferred.

How are storage locations organized?

Locations follow a hierarchy: Warehouse → Zone → Bin. Each bin has a defined capacity, and the system tracks current stock vs. available space in real time. Inward entries are assigned to specific bins, and outward dispatches deduct from the correct location automatically.

Does the system prevent overfilling a bin?

Yes. Ownership-first validation checks that the bin capacity is not exceeded before allowing a stock entry. If a transfer or inward entry would exceed bin capacity, the system blocks the operation and shows a validation error before any data is written.

Rent & Billing

How is rent calculated?

Rent is calculated based on the weight or quantity of stock stored and the duration (from inward date to outward or billing date). Rates are configured per product, per season, and per warehouse. You can bill on a monthly cycle or at the end of each season.

What happens to rent when stock is partially transferred?

Rent is split proportionally. When a farmer transfers a portion of their stock to another party, accumulated rent up to the transfer date is charged to the original owner. The new owner's rent accumulation starts from the transfer date on their share of the stock.

Can I configure different rent rates per product?

Yes. Rent rates are configured in the Seasons module per product category. You can set different rates for onions, potatoes, fruits, and other commodities. Seasonal pricing adjustments are also supported.

Are payment ledgers updated automatically?

Yes. Every inward entry, outward dispatch, rent charge, and payment is automatically posted to the farmer's ledger. The system maintains running balances showing payables and receivables in real time, with a complete transaction history.

Inward & Outward Operations

What information is recorded on inward entry?

Each inward entry records the farmer, product, variety, quantity (weight or bags), storage location (bin), entry date, and pricing. The entry auto-creates a stock lot, updates the location's utilization, and opens a payable ledger entry for the farmer.

How does the outward dispatch workflow work?

Outward starts with creating a challan for the farmer/owner. You select the stock lots to dispatch, specify quantities, and confirm. On completion, the system automatically deducts from bin stock, finalizes rent up to dispatch date, posts ledger entries, and records the challan for audit.

Can partial quantities be dispatched?

Yes. You can dispatch a partial quantity from any stock lot. The system splits the lot — deducting the dispatched amount from the bin and keeping the remaining stock active with its own ownership and rent tracking intact.
